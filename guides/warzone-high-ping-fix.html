How to Fix High Ping & Lag in Warzone — 2026 Guide

⚡ Quick Fix

Switch to wired Ethernet and change DNS to 8.8.8.8 / 1.1.1.1. This alone can drop ping by 20–50ms.

Why Is My Ping So High?

High ping in Warzone is caused by Wi-Fi instability, slow DNS, background downloads, ISP throttling, or distant servers.

Step-by-Step Fix

1

Use a Wired Connection

Wi-Fi adds latency and packet loss. Connect via Ethernet cable directly to your router. For long distances, use a Powerline adapter.

2

Change DNS Servers

Go to Network Settings → IPv4 → Properties. Set DNS to 8.8.8.8 / 8.8.4.4 (Google) or 1.1.1.1 / 1.0.0.1 (Cloudflare).

3

Close Background Apps

Pause downloads, close streaming apps (Netflix, YouTube), disable cloud sync (OneDrive, Dropbox), and limit other devices on your network.

4

Enable QoS on Router

Log into your router and find QoS settings. Prioritize your PC or Warzone ports (TCP/UDP: 3074).

5

Flush DNS and Reset Network

Run in CMD (Admin):

ipconfig /flushdns
netsh winsock reset
💎 Pro Tip

Upgrade to a Wi-Fi 6E router if wireless is your only option. If available, switch to a fiber ISP connection — going from 30–60ms (cable) to 5–15ms (fiber) is game-changing for competitive shooters.
